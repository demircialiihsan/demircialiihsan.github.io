About Me

My First Game

I started coding with Unity and C# during high school, and alongside my brother Said—an excellent 3D modeler and UI artist—we published our first game on Android during the summer before I began my Bachelor's in Computer Engineering at Istanbul Technical University in 2017. While the game is no longer available on the market, I recently uploaded it to here on itch.io for anyone who wants to check it out. Fair warning: I haven't touched it since the initial release almost a decade ago, and it's really hard to play!

Working at Garawell Games

During university years, I had the opportunity to work at the well-known Garawell Games, where I contributed to the very first prototype of what later became the widely recognized "Bridge Race". Over time, I worked on more than 10 hypercasual prototypes, gaining valuable experience collaborating with fellow developers and artists.

Co-founding Margin Games

After building my skills, I co-founded Margin Games with two partners. We started with a casual word game that featured a board and explosion mechanics similar to match-3 games. Through this experience, I gained hands-on knowledge of working with publishers, optimizing creatives, improving D1 retention to over 40%, maintaining a live game with updates, and running monetization tests.

From there, we shifted to hypercasual games, partnering with leading publishers and testing about 20 different projects. As the market evolved, we transitioned into hybrid-casual games, which proved to be a great move—one of our titles, "Happy Cubes - 2048", reached soft launch phase after strong initial test results, ultimately hitting 48.5% D1 retention. Today, the game has reached nearly 100,000 downloads only on Android.

The Next Step...

Now, I decided to open a new chapter in my career. I want to make a bigger impact, apply my skills where they matter most, and contribute more as I continue growing as a game developer.
